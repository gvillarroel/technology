# Architecture Review Charter

## Mission

The Architecture Review function exists to improve decision quality for technical changes that have broader-than-local impact. It is not a generic approval bottleneck. It should be engaged when a proposed change affects platform standards, integration contracts, security posture, or the long-term maintainability of the technology estate.

## Review Inputs

Submitters are expected to provide:

- the problem statement
- the proposed approach
- alternatives considered
- expected operational and organizational impact
- the decision needed from reviewers

## Review Outcomes

Review may result in:

1. approval with no conditions
2. approval with follow-up actions
3. request for revision
4. deferral pending more evidence

## Operating Principle

The review body should aim to make tradeoffs explicit. A weak or ambiguous decision is often more damaging than a strict one because it creates hidden variance across teams. When exceptions are approved, the reason and expiration horizon should be documented.
